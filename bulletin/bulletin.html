NOVEMBER 18, 2025

University Of Michigan’s Laser-Linked Satellites Will Distribute Critical Resources In Orbit

RResearchers at the University of Michigan have unveiled a cutting-edge concept that could reshape the way satellite constellations communicate: laser-based inter-satellite links. Instead of relying on traditional radio frequencies, these optical connections allow satellites to share data at lightning-fast speeds, reducing latency and bandwidth issues that often limit constellation performance. The result is a network that functions more like a synchronized digital web than isolated orbiting nodes.

by Earl Justine Mendoza

NOVEMBER 18, 2025

Mars-Bound Twins: NASA’s ESCAPADE Mission Prepares For Launch On New Glenn

NIn an ambitious step for interplanetary exploration, NASA is preparing to send twin spacecraft—nicknamed “Blue” and “Gold”—on the ESCAPADE mission to study Mars’ magnetosphere and atmospheric escape phenomena. The duo will be launched aboard the second flight of Blue Origin’s heavy-lift rocket New Glenn from Cape Canaveral, scheduled for early November 2025.
